Shanghai

SEE

The Bund

It'll be hard to go to SHanghai and not visit The Bund. It'll likely be one of your first stops. Walk next to the buildings of yester-year while looking across the river at the Shanghai skyline.

Xuhui Riverside Park

This one's off the tourist radar but is one of my favourite places in the city. Once and old shipping port, it has now been transformed into a long riverside park complete with climbing walls, grass areas and a skatepark. Bring some beers and grab a spot on the grass.

Shanghai Power Station of Art

For those looking for a bit of art and culture, this is a fantastic setting. An old power building turned museum, hosting some of the bigger exhibits which come through. Look out for any shows by Cai Guo-Qiang. Always a treat

EAT

Xinjiang Uyghur Restaurant

I'm not sure what I like the most about this place; the food, the black beer, or the crazy music/dance party that happens mid meal. Goat is definitely the specialtiy here and there is lots of it - they'll even give youu plastic gloves so you can shovel it into your mouth.

Lotus Eatery

Lotus Eatery is a always a go-to place for group dinners, birthdays and goodbyes. Lots of goodbyes. The food is Yunnanese (south east China) and seems to cross more standard Chinese food with Thai/Burmese. Order the fried goat cheese!

Uncle Cha's

This is the place to go for youur HK food fix. It's cheap and simple and very popular (be prepared to wait in line a bit). Classics like HK duck and crispy pork as well as some of the best fried chicken in town.

DRINK

Constellation Bar

Constellation used to be one of those "in the know" places which everybody knew about. And it is still going strong due to the great quality drinks in a cool environment. Get a Basil Mint. Enjoy.

Beer Lady

Originally a small beer shop/copy shop, this place expanded quickly and now has three branches. All have thousands of import beers and tables set up so you can drink on the sopt. Cheap fo sure.

DANCE

Dada Bar

A small bar with dj nights which expanded to be a club, Dada bar now has a second location in Beijing and both are rocking. As I lived around the corner from this place for years I've definitely spent my fair share of time here.

Elevator

Named after the ridiculously small elevator which takes you up to the 4th floor, Elevator was opened by Dj mau mau a couuple of years back and boasts great local and international talent. Get your house and techno fix here.

Craft

I'm actually not sure if Craft is still open, but at one time was a very laid back bar/club where you could get your disco/rare grooves on.
